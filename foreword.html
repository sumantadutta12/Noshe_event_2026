Foreword

As we stand at the intersection of an evolving industrial landscape and a world grappling with the pressing realities of climate change, the importance of occupational safety and health (OSH) has never been more profound. Workplaces globally are facing unprecedented challenges—ranging from the disruptive impacts of technological advancements to the health risks exacerbated by environmental shifts. According to the International Labour Organization (ILO), over 2.3 million people globally succumb to work-related accidents or diseases every year. In this volatile environment, ensuring the safety, health, and well-being of workers is not just an operational necessity; it is a fundamental pillar for sustainable growth and societal resilience.

In India, this challenge is particularly pronounced. The vastness of our workforce, spanning both organized and unorganized sectors, presents unique complexities. Millions of workers across diverse industries—from manufacturing and agriculture to emerging sectors like technology—are exposed to varying degrees of risk. Rapid urbanization, industrialization, and climate-related phenomena such as extreme heat, pollution, and shifting weather patterns have further compounded the vulnerabilities faced by our workforce. The magnitude of this challenge calls for a concerted, multifaceted response that integrates best practices in safety with innovative, forward- thinking approaches tailored to India's specific needs.

Under the visionary leadership of our Honourable Prime Minister Shri. Narendra Modi Ji, the Government of India has recognized the critical importance of OSH in driving national progress. Through transformative policies and initiatives, the government has underscored its commitment to creating safe and healthy workplaces for all. From stringent safety regulations to the promotion of sustainable industrial practices, these efforts are laying the groundwork for a safer, more resilient workforce that can thrive in the face of both present and future challenges.

Aligned with this national imperative, the Society of Occupational Safety, Health & Environment Professionals (SOSHE) is emerging as a key force dedicated to revolutionizing the OSH landscape in India. With a mission to foster safety culture and promote sustainability, SOSHE is committed to addressing the most pressing OSH challenges through advocacy, capacity-building, and the promotion of global best practices. Our aim is to not only raise the bar on safety standards but to also drive a cultural shift in how we perceive and implement worker safety and health at every level of industry.

As part of our ongoing initiatives, SOSHECON, the annual flagship conference of SOSHE, stands as a pivotal platform for advancing this agenda. Bringing together policymakers, thought leaders, industry stalwarts, experts, and OSH practitioners, SOSHECON 2026 will explore the theme "Creating a Resilient Workplace: Sustainable Health, Safety and Environmental Strategies for a Changing Climate".

The conference will serve as a catalyst for knowledge-sharing, collaboration, and the development of actionable strategies that will shape the future of OSH in India and beyond, ensuring that we build not just healthy workplaces, but resilient communities that are equipped to meet the challenges of tomorrow.

Join us to delve into actionable insights, foster a shared vision & inspire a collective commitment to building a resilient and prosperous Viksit Bharat!
